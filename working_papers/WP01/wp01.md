## 1. Introduction

African judicial systems face a dual transformation: on one hand, growing pressure to modernize their administrative and informational infrastructures; on the other, the emergence of artificial intelligence (AI) technologies whose deployment raises unprecedented challenges of governance, equity, and accountability.

This working paper explores the question of judicial data governance in the African context, drawing on the Ivorian experience — notably through the Computerized System for Data and Prosecution Operations Management (SYGIDOP) — to propose an analytical framework transferable to other jurisdictions on the continent.

**Objectives of this paper:**
- Identify structural challenges of data governance in African judicial institutions.
- Propose a conceptual framework articulating data governance, responsible AI, and the digital transformation of justice.
- Formulate operational recommendations for policymakers and practitioners in the judicial sector.
