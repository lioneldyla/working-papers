# Abstract — WP01

## Judicial Data Governance and Responsible Artificial Intelligence
### An Analytical Framework for African Judicial Institutions

**Author:** Yoboukoua Lionel Arnaud DJORO  
**Affiliation:** National Institute for Judicial Training (INFJ), Cote d'Ivoire  
**Date:** June 2026 | **Version:** v0.1 — Draft

---

## Abstract

African judicial institutions face a dual transformation: the digital modernization of their administrative systems and the emergence of artificial intelligence (AI) in the judicial sphere. While these developments open significant prospects for efficiency and access to justice, they also raise critical issues of data governance, algorithmic fairness, and institutional accountability.

This working paper proposes an analytical framework for conceptualizing judicial data governance as a prerequisite for the responsible deployment of AI in Africa's justice sector. Drawing on the Ivorian experience — particularly through the Computerized System for Data and Prosecution Operations Management (SYGIDOP) and the judicial statistical yearbooks of 2019 and 2023 — it identifies structural challenges in data governance within African jurisdictions and links these challenges to the requirements of responsible AI (transparency, explainability, non-discrimination, accountability).

The paper mobilizes the Judicial Data Governance Framework (JDGF), developed by the author, as an analytical and institutional diagnostic tool. It formulates operational recommendations for policymakers and practitioners in the African judicial sector, with a view to transferring good practices across jurisdictions on the continent.

**Keywords:** judicial data governance, responsible AI, digital transformation of justice, Africa, Cote d'Ivoire, SYGIDOP, JDGF
